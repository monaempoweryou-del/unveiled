Our Story

A movement that never stopped.

Founded in Los Angeles in 1979 by Ron Allen, The Child Super STAR Program was built on one belief: every child deserves someone who believes they can become great.

It began with ballgame tickets for kids who had never seen the field, hospital visits for those who needed a champion, and a founder who refused to let a single child feel forgotten. Athletes lent their names. The Los Angeles Times told the story. The Mayor's office took notice.

More than forty years later, the doors are opening again for a new generation of young stars.

Founder & DirectorRon Allen
Meet The Founder

The man who believed first.

Ron Allen has spent his life giving children something money cannot buy: opportunity, confidence, mentorship, and a place to belong. He drove kids to practices, sat with families in hospital rooms, and made sure no child in his program ever felt like they came second.

What he built in 1979 was never really about sports. It was about showing a young person that someone was in their corner, for good.

A superstar isn't created by talent alone. A superstar is built through confidence, discipline, and someone believing in them.Ron Allen, Founder
