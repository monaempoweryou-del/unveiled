## 1. Positioning Statement

**One sentence:**
> *The Law Offices of Shani Gabay is the Israeli community's own attorney in Los Angeles — a Hebrew-, Spanish-, and English-speaking lawyer in the heart of Encino who personally handles immigration, citizenship, injury, family, and real estate matters, 24/7, so you never have to navigate the American legal system alone or in a second language.*

**The emotional hook (what stops the scroll):**
> **"מדברים את השפה שלך. / We speak your language."**
> Not just Hebrew — your situation, your culture, your worry. One lawyer who gets it, handles everything, and answers the phone herself.

**Why this wins (traceable to research):** The English immigration field on Ventura Blvd is brutally saturated — Root Law (72 reviews, "95% approval"), Raynor (55, since 1997), Sidman (since 1983). Competing head-on is a multi-year losing battle (Research §3, §4). The **open, defensible lane** is *"the Valley's Hebrew-speaking, full-service Israeli attorney"* — no competitor owns it (Sheff is Hebrew but Beverly Hills + corporate; the Encino specialists are English-only). The emotional driver is **relief + belonging** (Research §5): the Israeli immigrant facing USCIS in a second language feels anxiety and isolation; "you're not alone, and you don't have to do this in English" is the message that converts.

## 2. Site Structure

Single-page site, **bilingual Hebrew (RTL) + English** with a prominent language toggle in the header. **Default landing language: Hebrew (RTL)** — the lead came through the Hebrew funnel and Hebrew-speaking clients are the strategic target; English is one click away for everyone else. *(Open item from Research §6 — default language to be confirmed with client; building Hebrew-first per the funnel source.)*

Sections in order:

### Section 1 — Hero
- **Purpose:** In 5 seconds, communicate "this is the Israeli community's lawyer, she speaks your language, and help is one call away." Establish the niche and trigger relief.
- **Headline direction:** Lead with the bilingual cultural hook. Hebrew headline primary in RTL view; English equivalent in EN view. Angle: *"Your attorney in Los Angeles — in your language."* Subhead names the breadth (immigration, citizenship, injury, family, real estate) and the personal/24-7 promise.
- **Content needs:** Professional attorney headshot of Shani (critical — counters the "no photo" gap, Research §6). Language toggle (HE/EN). Trilingual badge cue ("עברית · English · Español"). Free-consultation offer visible. Click-to-call phone (818-961-4861). Clean, confident type over deep-navy background.
- **CTA (primary):** "Free Consultation — Call Now / ייעוץ חינם — התקשרו עכשיו" (click-to-call) + secondary "Send a Message" anchor to contact form.

### Section 2 — Trust Bar
- **Purpose:** Immediate credibility scaffolding — the single biggest gap on the current site (Research §2).
- **Headline direction:** None / minimal — this is a horizontal strip of proof.
- **Content needs:** Four–five trust chips: **~10 years licensed in California** · **Trilingual (Hebrew/English/Spanish)** · **Available 24/7** · **Free Consultation** · **5.0 ratings (Avvo / 100% Facebook)**. AILA badge *if confirmed* (open item, Research §6). Clean disciplinary record cue.
- **CTA:** None (visual reinforcement only).

### Section 3 — Services / Practice Areas
- **Purpose:** Show the full-service breadth as *convenience* — "one trusted Israeli lawyer for all of life's legal needs" — not a scattered menu (Research §3 gap #4, §5).
- **Headline direction:** *"One lawyer. Everything life throws at you."* Frame immigration as the flagship, the rest as the reassurance that she stays your lawyer beyond the green card.
- **Content needs:** Five practice-area cards, immigration-led:
  1. **Immigration Law** (flagship — green cards, visas, family-based)
  2. **U.S. Citizenship & Naturalization**
  3. **Personal Injury**
  4. **Family Law**
  5. **Real Estate**
  Each card: short, plain-language description of *the client's problem solved*, in Hebrew/English. *(Criminal defense — ~20% of practice — held back pending client confirmation, Research §1/§6 open item; do not surface by default.)*
- **CTA:** "Not sure where you fit? Call for a free consult."

### Section 4 — Why Shani / The Differentiator
- **Purpose:** Convert the niche into the explicit reason to choose her over the 72-review volume mills. This is the heart of the page.
- **Headline direction:** *"Not a law firm that processes you. A lawyer who understands you."* Contrast the personal, in-your-language, A-to-Z handling against the faceless volume shops (Research §3 gap #3).
- **Content needs:** 3–4 pillars with short copy + icons:
  - **In your language** — Hebrew, English, Spanish; explain your case the way you actually think about it.
  - **Handled personally, A-to-Z** — Shani works your case herself; you're not handed to a paralegal or a call center.
  - **Around the clock** — genuine 24/7 availability; she answers.
  - **In the heart of your community** — on Ventura Blvd, Encino, where the Israeli community lives and works.
- **CTA:** "Talk to Shani directly — free consultation."

### Section 5 — About Shani
- **Purpose:** Humanize hard. Put a face, a story, and credentials behind the promise (Research §6, Key Rec #3).
- **Headline direction:** *"Meet Shani Gabay."* Warm, first-person voice ("I came here too / I understand the journey"), grounded in credentials.
- **Content needs:** Larger attorney photo. Short bio: licensed in California since 2015 (~10 years), clean record, trilingual, rooted in the Israeli community of the Valley. First-person, personal tone ("Call me today"). Cultural-fit narrative without overclaiming.
- **CTA:** Soft — leads into testimonials.

### Section 6 — Testimonials / Reviews
- **Purpose:** Social proof — upgrade from the current site's weak anonymous initials (Research §2).
- **Headline direction:** *"What clients say."* Emphasize real outcomes and being understood.
- **Content needs:** Testimonials **with names + stars + source attribution (Yelp/Avvo/Facebook/Google)** — a clear upgrade from "E.D." initials. Pull from existing 5.0 Avvo / 100% Facebook recommendation base. *(Phase 2+ asset ask: gather named, photo'd reviews with stars; seed Google Business Profile — Research §5.)* Include at least one Hebrew-language testimonial in the HE view if available.
- **CTA:** "Read more reviews" (link to Yelp/Google) + "Add your story — book a consult."

### Section 7 — Service Area
- **Purpose:** Anchor local relevance and SEO; reassure she serves the whole community, not just Encino (Research §1, §4).

### Section 8 — Contact / Final CTA

- **Content needs:** Click-to-call phone (818-961-4861), email (law@shanigabay.com), short contact form (name, phone, language preference, brief message), office address + hours ("24/7 availability"), Facebook + Yelp links. **Remove all dead/legacy links** (Google+, defunct since 2019 — Research §2). ## 4. Design Direction

**Color palette (decided):**
- **Primary — Deep Navy / Midnight Blue `#0F2742`** — authority, trust, stability (legal standard; Research §6).
- **Secondary — Warm White / Soft Warm Gray `#F7F5F1` / `#E8E6E1`** — clean, premium, readable backgrounds.
- **Accent — Warm Gold / Brass `#C9A24B`** — premium, success, optimism; pairs naturally with an Israeli/Jewish visual sensibility without being literal.
- **Text — Charcoal `#1A1F26`** on light; warm white `#FAF9F7` on navy.
- *Restrained, optional cultural nod:* a sparing touch of Israeli-flag blue/white may appear as a micro-accent (e.g., language-toggle state), **never** as a theme — avoid looking like a flag (Research §6).
- **Explicitly avoid** the cold, generic blue-and-gray template look of the current site.

**Typography feel:** Modern, confident, trustworthy. Large, assertive headline type; highly readable body. **Must include a quality Hebrew typeface** that pairs with the Latin face and renders RTL cleanly (e.g., a modern Hebrew sans for headings, readable Hebrew serif/sans for body). Generous whitespace.

**Image style:** Professional, human, warm-but-premium. Real attorney headshot of Shani (hero + about). Office/Ventura Blvd exterior or interior. Optional authentic-feeling lifestyle/community imagery (families, citizenship-ceremony warmth) — sourced stock acceptable if it doesn't look generic. Clean, not stocky; faces and warmth over abstract gavel/scales clichés.

**Overall vibe in 3 words:** **Trusted · Personal · Bilingual.**

## 5. Conversion Strategy

**Primary CTA:** **"Free Consultation — Call Now" (click-to-call, 818-961-4861)**, repeated in hero, sticky header/mobile bar, after each major section, and in the contact block. Free consult removes the entry barrier (price is secondary to trust — Research §4); phone-first matches her personal, 24/7 promise.

**Secondary CTA:** **"Send a Message"** — short bilingual contact form (name, phone, preferred language, brief message) for visitors who won't call first. Email `law@shanigabay.com` as tertiary.

**Trust signals to emphasize (in priority order):**
1. **Language + cultural fit** — Hebrew/English/Spanish; the bilingual RTL site itself is the strongest trust signal no competitor offers (Research §3, §6).
2. **Personal, A-to-Z handling + 24/7 availability** — warmth the volume shops can't claim.
3. **Credentials** — ~10 years licensed, clean disciplinary record, 5.0 Avvo / 100% Facebook; AILA badge *if confirmed*.
4. **Named testimonials with stars + source.**
5. **Community location** — Ventura Blvd, Encino.

**Urgency / scarcity angle:** Soft and authentic, not manufactured. Lean on **"available 24/7 — call today"** and the genuine stakes of immigration timelines (USCIS deadlines, court dates) to create natural urgency — *"Don't wait until a deadline becomes a crisis."* No fake countdowns or false scarcity (would undercut the trust positioning).
