Shani Gabay is a solo immigration attorney in Encino, CA (the heart of Los Angeles's Israeli community) who came in through our **Hebrew-language** funnel. She is a **Hebrew-, Spanish-, and English-speaking** lawyer, licensed in California since 2015 (~10 years), serving primarily the Israeli/Hebrew-speaking community of the San Fernando Valley.

## 2. Existing Website Analysis — https://www.shanigabay.com/

**Verdict: dated single-page brochure site. Design quality 4/10.**

**Structure (appears to be a one-page / few-page site):**
- Welcome / Home — H1: "WELCOME TO THE LAW OFFICES OF SHANI GABAY"
- Areas of Practice (4 areas shown: Immigration, Citizenship & Naturalization, Personal Injury, Family Law)
- Reviews / Testimonials (5 short initials-only quotes: E.D., B.S., S.A., N.K., A.W.)
- Contact + Questions form
- Repeated CTAs: "Get FREE CONSULTATION," "Call me today for a free consultation"

**Contact:** Phone 818-961-4861 (repeated), law@shanigabay.com, 16027 Ventura Blvd. Social links to Facebook, **Twitter, LinkedIn, Google+ (defunct since 2019)**, Yelp.

**Issues identified:**
- **English only** — despite the firm's entire edge being the Hebrew-speaking community. No Hebrew, no RTL version. This is the single biggest missed opportunity.
- **Dead/legacy elements** — Google+ link (service shut down 2019) signals the site hasn't been meaningfully updated in 5+ years.
- **Generic differentiators** — "Secured Privacy / Performance / Anywhere in CA" are vague and say nothing the visitor cares about; no proof, no specifics.
- **No credibility scaffolding** — no years-in-practice stated, no attorney bio/photo, no bar credentials, no case results, no AILA membership badge, no "se habla / מדברים עברית" trust cue.
- **Anonymous testimonials** — initials only ("E.D."), no stars, no source attribution; weak vs. competitors showing 50+ verified Yelp reviews.
- **Thin content / weak SEO** — H1 is a generic "Welcome" line (wastes the most valuable on-page SEO real estate); no evidence of practice-area landing pages to rank for searches like "Hebrew speaking immigration lawyer Encino."

**What works:** Clear, repeated calls to action; free-consultation offer is prominent; phone number is everywhere; tone is personal and approachable (good raw material to keep).

**Mobile / speed:** Could not benchmark precisely, but the design vintage (Google+ era, brochure layout) strongly suggests a legacy template that is at best marginally mobile-responsive and not performance-optimized. Treat as a rebuild candidate, not a refresh.

**Competitiveness:** **English immigration search in Encino is extremely competitive** — established firms with 50–70+ reviews and 20–40 years tenure dominate the maps pack and directories. Winning there head-on is a multi-year SEO/review battle. **Hebrew-language search and Israeli-community referral, by contrast, are wide open** — far fewer competitors, much warmer intent, and a defensible niche. This is where to concentrate.

**Directories / platforms that matter here:**
- **Yelp** (she has a listing: 17 reviews) — heavily used in LA for lawyer selection; needs more volume.
- **Google Business Profile** — the single most important asset; must be claimed, fully built, and review-fueled.
- **Avvo** (6.2 "Good," 5.0 from 3 reviews), **Martindale, Lawyers.com, Justia** — present but thin/incomplete (Martindale has no photo, no reviews).
- **Facebook** — 100% recommend / 13 reviews; the natural hub for Israeli-community word-of-mouth.

**What the local (Israeli) customer cares about, in order:**
1. **Trust + cultural understanding** — "she's one of us, she gets my situation, I can explain it in Hebrew."
2. **Accessibility / responsiveness** — 24/7, answers the phone personally, no cold call-center.
3. **Proven outcomes** — did she actually get people their green cards/citizenship.
4. **Convenience** — one lawyer for immigration, injury, family, real estate.
5. Price is secondary to trust (free consult removes the entry barrier).
